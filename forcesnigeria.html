NIGERIA FORCES

EFCC

Name & Role

  • The EFCC is Nigeria's premier anti-graft and financial crime enforcement agency, mandated to investigate,
    prosecute, and prevent offences like advance fee fraud (commonly known as “419 fraud”), money laundering,
    and other financial crimes.

Origins & Timeline

  • Established by an Act of the National Assembly on 12 December 2002, the EFCC began operations on 13 April
    2003, under President Olusegun Obasanjo and its first chairman, Mallam Nuhu Ribadu. This move responded to
    international pressure—particularly from the Financial Action Task Force (FATF)—on weak anti-money-launde-
    ring measures in Nigeria

DSS

Identity & Origin

  • The DSS Legally established in 1986 under the National Security Agencies Act as the State Security Ser-
    vice (SSS) to handle domestic intelligence, following the split of the old National Security Organization
    (NSO) into three entities.

Mandate & Responsibilities

  • Preventing and detecting threats to internal security—including terrorism, espionage, sabotage, and
    separatist movements
  • Protecting classified internal matters and safeguarding sensitive government facilities, officials, and
    visiting dignitaries
  • Advising the government on national security matters DevelopmentAid

NPF

What Is the NPF?

  • The NPFis the federal law enforcement agency with exclusive jurisdiction nationwide, established to
    uphold internal security and enforce law and order. It’s constitutionally backed under Section 214 of
    the 1999 Constitution (as amended) and governed by the Nigeria Police (Establishment) Act of 2020

Historical Background

  • Its roots trace back to colonial-era paramilitary units, including the Hausa Constabulary (1820), Lagos
    Police (1896), and others, which were consolidated over time into a national force by 1930

Size & Leadership

  • As of 2021, the NPF maintained approximately 371,800 officers, with governmental plans to expand to
    650,000 by adding 280,000 recruits

Mission, Vision & Values

  • Vision:Foster a safer Nigeria that supports economic growth and security for all

  • Mission:Collaborate with citizens and security agencies to gather intelligence.
    Address crime’s root causes and prosecute offenders fairly.
    Promote officer welfare and capacity.
